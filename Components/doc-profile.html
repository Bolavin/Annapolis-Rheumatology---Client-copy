Biography

Respect all patients, their culture and beliefs.

Dr. Saade is an internist, rheumatologist, and immunologist. He firmly believes in giving back to the community through his active involvement in research, education, and leadership roles—a commitment reflected in his impressive career trajectory. 

Upon completion of his Internal Medicine training, Dr. Saade served as a chief medical resident, and appointed as the Co-Chair of Chief Resident Association of Baltimore in 2017. His distinguished service in this role was recognized with an outstanding service award by the Maryland ACP.

 Following his tenure as chief resident, Dr. Saade received a faculty appointment as an assistant professor at Georgetown University and served as a faculty physician at the MedStar Health Internal Medicine residency program. During this time, he completed two years of training in Leadership Education and Development (LEAD) and earned a Medical Education Research Certificate (MERC). After these achievements, he accepted the role of associate program director for the Internal Medicine (IM) residency program at MedStar Health – Baltimore, serving for six months before embarking on advanced training in rheumatology and immunology.

 Dr. Saade extensively trained in rheumatology and immunology at the University of Pittsburgh, gaining expertise in both common and rare specialties. His robust training across five hospitals and more than ten specialized clinics prepared him to manage complex cases confidently.

 In addition to his clinical work, Dr. Saade has made substantial contributions to research. He led a large-scale myositis research project, working alongside world-renowned experts in the field. He also initiated quality improvement projects aimed at the early identification and swift implementation of treatments for life-threatening autoimmune diseases. His hard work led to his appointment as chief fellow, a testament to his expertise and leadership
